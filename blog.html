Honeymoon in Paradise

Something I really struggle with (or 2 things) is taking my camera out on general outings - whether it be by myself or with others. The second thing is bridging the gap between professional and personal on platforms such as this.
I’m always unsure of what level of professionalism people expect from me and what EVEN defines professionalism?! I could go on but that’s probably another tangent for another day!
Long story short - I’m working on improving both of these things by keeping my camera with me more often during my down time and letting you all have an insight into the awkward odd ball I am the majority of the time!

With these things in mind, I wanted to write a blog entirely on the breathtakingly beautiful experience of my recent honeymoon in Tulum, Mexico. For a multitude of reasons I’ve never really traveled much but now I find myself in a position to do so and I am so grateful for the few but amazing places I have seen! Mexico has been one of my top three destinations to go to for awhile now and what could be better than visiting a tropical and cultural paradise? Well seeing it all with my fantastic husband of course! For anyone that hasn’t been before I highly recommend it and it is totally worth the crazy jet lag coming home!

My Tulum top tips!

1. Cenotes! Cenotes! CENOTES! - oh Lordy! These were an experience on another level! I am not a strong swimmer and to anyone else that is not, please don’t let it put you off. The majority of them have equipment you can hire or just borrow for snorkelling and the beauty of them is just awe inspiring!

2. Vegan paradise! - Mexico turned out to be a vegan paradise! Well Tulum at least (not sure about everywhere else). Killian and myself love eating yummy food together and with Kill being vegan we often find ourselves in interesting places and Tulum was chock a block!

3. Bug spray - Pffffft I feel like I got more bites when I put spray on but oh well. You deffo need at LEAST two bottles a week. You are going to the jungle.

4. Tulum town - Tulum is made up of the beach strip and the town, both are pretty touristy but the beach strip is the hipster international capital! We only went into the town once at the end of our time in Tulum and how I wish we’d gone earlier! Sooo much more to see, so much more to eat, and a million times cheaper. Top money saving tip!

A busy day on the run up to Christmas!

I’m happy to say that even though we’re out of wedding season it’s still business as usual! As we get closer to Christmas I’m seeing an increase of family sessions which is always a refreshing a fun change of scenery. It also gives me the opportunity to catch up with past clients on the next chapter of their lives.

Family and kids sessions can be a perfect gift for Christmas, whether it be the shoot itself as an experience or prints for loved ones. I want to share with you two different types of sessions I experienced this November, plus something totally different!

First, I spent a drizzly Saturday morning with, quite frankly, a dazzling family. The Rogers/Fishburn clan are a beautiful bunch of brothers and sisters who got me involved to photograph them for their mum and grand parents for Christmas. As I already mentioned, the weather was pretty diabolical but we all wrapped up and soon enough we hardly noticed!

Sometimes a prop like an umbrella can be a great little addition to a playful shoot, especially with youngsters! We shot in the scenic belle vue park in Newport, a place where they all had spent a lot of time growing up, and a great spot for a rainy shoot with that band stand being a life saver when the rain got a little too heavy to handle. A great day! And probably one of my all time favourite shoots, I love working with larger groups! So many more options and ideas!

The second shoot I’d like to share with you today is something a little outside of the norm for me but a success and something I hope to experiment more with in the future. With the welsh weather being a fickle mistress, location photography can be tricky and choc full of postponements. One crazy Thursday was no exception, with it being a little too wet and windy for my camera to handle. I was meeting up with Vicky and her little girl Thea for some super Christmassy shots for Theas grandparents.

With the ideas we had now out the window, some fast thinking, a bit of DIY and handy collection of tat allowed me to set up a small studio area fit for a toddler inside from the cold. I, myself am not one for studio shoots, having worked in a studio setting before, I find them a little too restrictive. This however was the perfect occasion for the little fiery lass who spent the majority of the time chasing my dog and throwing tinsel on me than she did say in front of the tree! It was a great experiment though and another feather to my boa! Giving me the option of offering indoor sessions for babies and toddlers.

I’m so grateful for experiences like these, meeting new people, working with fantastic individuals and challenging myself with new creative responses! If any of the work shown throughout this blog post has inspired you to join me for a great day out of family fun then please get in touch via email. I’d be so excited to hear from you!

Autumnal Engagement - Kasia & Caesar

This year has been a photographers dream weather wise! Long hot summers and a crisp colourful autumn! I can only hope for the winter wonderland of my dreams (and an awesome couple to play around in the snow).

Autumn brought me one of my favourite photoshoots of the year, an engagement session with a couple I met in early October. Kasia & Caesar were an amazingly fun couple to work with, quickly getting into their own stride and showing me their personalities.

We decided to shoot at St. Fagans national history museum on what must of somehow been their busiest day of year!? A little deterred by the amount of people, I decided it would be best to seek quieter ground and we absconded to the castle. The first 20 minutes of any shoot can be the most important, and I pride myself on making people feel as comfortable and relaxed in front of the camera as possible.

Once we found a couple of quiet spots by the water and the guys loosened up, we decided to be a little more adventurous and autumnal! Whats a good autumn shoot without playing around with some leaves! Spontaneous shots are some of my favourites.Not without their complications but you really get to see the joy and personality in the people and the place.

After this, we were on a roll! The sun was shining, the leaves were crisp, spirits were high! And no amount of crowds or photo bombers could stop us!
